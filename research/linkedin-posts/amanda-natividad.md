### Summary

Amanda explains that marketing strategy should begin with understanding how a company grows. Rather than starting with tactics, marketers should identify whether the business relies on discovery-led, product-led, or sales-led growth and align content efforts accordingly.

Early marketing wins help establish credibility and internal trust.
Strategic alignment is more important than following generic best practices.

### Summary

Amanda challenges the traditional concept of a single launch day. She argues that successful launches are gradual rollouts that start with highly engaged customers before expanding to broader audiences, creating proof and momentum before public promotion.

### Key Takeaways

Product launches should be treated as multi-stage rollouts.
Early adopters generate valuable social proof.
Customer advocacy increases launch credibility.
Building momentum before public announcements improves adoption.
