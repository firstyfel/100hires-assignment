Research Findings

Research Topic

LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

Experts Reviewed

Justin Welsh
Amanda Natividad
Chris Walker

Recurring Content Patterns

1. Personal Experience Builds Credibility

Both Justin Welsh and Amanda Natividad frequently use personal experiences to support their insights. Instead of presenting abstract theories, they explain lessons learned through real situations.

Examples:

Justin Welsh discussing leaving a high-paying corporate role.
Amanda Natividad discussing her career decisions and marketing experience.

2. Educational Content Outperforms Promotion

All three experts focus on teaching before selling. Most content provides practical advice, frameworks, or lessons before introducing any product, service, or resource.

3. Frameworks Improve Engagement

Complex ideas are simplified into frameworks, lists, and structured explanations.

Examples:

Justin Welsh’s 7 capacities.
Amanda Natividad’s marketing strategy framework.
Chris Walker’s GTM frameworks.

4. Strong Opinions Attract Attention

Many successful posts begin by challenging conventional wisdom.

Examples:

“I don’t believe in launch days.”
“The timeline you’re panicking about is fake.”
Criticism of traditional GTM approaches.

5. Long-Term Thinking Is a Common Theme

All three experts emphasize sustainable growth, compounding results, and strategic decision-making rather than short-term tactics.

Key Learnings for B2B SaaS LinkedIn Strategy

Lead with education rather than promotion.
Use personal experiences to make ideas memorable.
Present insights through frameworks and lists.
Challenge common assumptions to create engagement.
Focus on long-term value rather than growth hacks.

Initial Conclusion

Successful B2B SaaS creators consistently combine expertise, storytelling, and structured educational content. Their content builds trust by helping audiences solve problems rather than directly selling products or services.
